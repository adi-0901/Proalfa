Part A — The Accounts Role at Proalfa Dynamic

1. Responsibilities Overview

The accounts team is responsible for the full financial health of Proalfa Dynamic — from client billing and vendor payments to statutory compliance and management reporting. The ERP dashboard supports but does not replace your judgment; you must cross-check the system against physical records and bank statements regularly.

Golden rule: The ERP is your working view. The primary records are your physical vouchers, bank passbook, and tally/accounting software. Always reconcile both. Any discrepancy must be investigated and resolved — never ignore it.

Daily / Weekly Routine (Physical)

  • Record all cash receipts and payments in petty cash register on the same day
  • Attach physical bill/receipt to every payment voucher before filing
  • Log every bank transaction in your bank reconciliation sheet (weekly)
  • Stamp "PAID" with date on vendor bills once payment is made
  • File new sales invoices in sequence on the day of issue
  • Check incoming cheques for post-dated status; log separately

Before paying any vendor: Check if TDS is applicable. If yes, deduct at source, deposit by 7th of next month, and issue Form 16A within 15 days of filing the quarterly return. Vendors will follow up — track it proactively.

GST TCS vs TDS — Quick Distinction

  • TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) — You deduct from vendor payment. Income Tax Act.
  • TCS (Tax Collected at Source) — Seller collects from buyer on certain goods. Applies above ₹50L in a year under Income Tax.
  • GST TDS — Certain government bodies deduct 2% GST TDS on payments to you. You can see & claim it in your GST portal credit ledger.

5. Banking & Vendor Relations

Bank Account Management

  • Maintain separate records for each bank account (current account, CC account, OD facility).
  • Reconcile each account statement at month-end. Every entry must match your vouchers.
  • Keep net banking credentials secure. Report any suspicious transaction to management immediately.
  • For cheque payments: always cross cheques "A/c Payee Only". Log cheque number, date, amount, and payee in the cheque register before dispatch.
  • Maintain a post-dated cheque (PDC) register if clients issue PDCs. Present on due date; mark in ERP on realisation.

CC / OD Facility

  • Check the CC/OD balance daily against the sanctioned limit. Never let utilisation exceed the limit without management approval.
  • Interest is debited by the bank monthly — record it in ERP under Credit Line tab → Drawdown Log.
  • Bank may ask for updated stock statements (for CC against stock) — prepare and submit within the deadline given by the bank (usually monthly or quarterly).
  • Renewal of CC/OD facility is typically annual — start the renewal process 2 months before expiry.

Vendor Payment Discipline

  • Review payables weekly — identify bills due in the next 15 days and plan payments.
  • Always obtain a receipt/acknowledgement for every payment made (even NEFT — print the transaction confirmation).
  • For new vendors, verify bank account details directly (call/email) before the first payment. Fraud via fake account change requests is common.
  • If a vendor disputes a payment, produce the payment voucher + bank statement entry. Do not pay again without a full reconciliation.

Good habit: Every Friday, review all overdue receivables and escalate to management if any client is more than 30 days past due. Proactive follow-up prevents bad debts.

Part B — Finance ERP Guide: End to End

The Finance Dashboard is at /staff/finance.html. Access requires the finance password. The dashboard is divided into 8 tabs. Each tab is covered below with step-by-step instructions.

Login: Open /staff/finance.html → enter finance password. Your session is remembered in the browser (sessionStorage) until you close the tab. Do not share the URL or password with anyone outside the finance team.

Step 1 — Daily Overview Routine

Start every working day on the Overview tab. It is your morning financial briefing.

What the Overview Tab Shows

  • Stat cards (top row): Total outstanding receivables, upcoming payables, current month revenue (from P&L), and cash/bank balance.
  • Top Overdue Receivables table: Clients with the largest overdue amounts — call or email these parties today if overdue > 7 days.
  • Upcoming Payables (30 days): Vendor bills due in the next 30 days — plan your cash outflows.
  • Recent Vouchers: Last few transactions entered — quick audit trail to confirm yesterday's entries are correct.

Daily Morning Checklist

  • Open Finance Dashboard → Overview tab. Note receivables outstanding and payables due this week.
  • Check bank balance in net banking. Compare with ERP cash flow opening balance.
  • Flag any client overdue > 30 days to management via WhatsApp/email.
  • Identify any vendor payment due today — initiate NEFT or prepare cheque.
  • Check CC/OD utilisation (Credit Line tab) — ensure within limit.

Recording a Collection (Payment Received)

4
Update cash flow. The receipt should automatically appear in Cash Flow → Inflows. Verify it is reflected correctly.

Aging analysis: The Receivables tab shows how long each invoice has been outstanding. Review the aging weekly. Escalate invoices older than 60 days to management — these may need legal notice or written follow-up.

Step 3 — Payables: Vendor Bills & Payments

The Payables tab tracks all amounts Proalfa owes to vendors and suppliers. Staying on top of this protects your credit relationships and avoids late payment charges.

Recording a Vendor Bill

1
Receive the bill. Vendor sends an invoice/bill. Verify the bill against the purchase order or delivery challan. Check amounts, GST, and GSTIN.
2
Check TDS applicability. Is this vendor subject to TDS (Section 194C for contractors, 194J for professionals)? If yes, note the TDS amount to deduct.
3
Add to ERP. In the Payables tab, click + Add Bill. Fill Party/Vendor, Bill No., Bill Date, Due Date, Amount. Save. The bill now appears in upcoming payables.
4
File physical bill. Stamp the bill with "RECEIVED" and today's date. File it in the Purchase Bill File.

Making a Payment

1
Prepare payment. Initiate NEFT/RTGS in net banking (or prepare cheque). If TDS applies, pay the net amount (bill amount minus TDS). Note the UTR/cheque number.
2
Mark as paid in ERP. In Payables tab, find the bill. Click Mark Paid. Enter payment date and amount. If TDS was deducted, note it in the narration field.
3
Write payment voucher. Record in Payment Voucher Book: vendor name, bill number, gross amount, TDS amount, net paid, UTR/cheque number, date. Attach bank confirmation printout.
4
Stamp physical bill "PAID" with date and payment reference. This prevents accidental duplicate payment.

Duplicate payment risk: Before making any payment, search the Payables tab for the bill number. If it is already marked "Paid," stop. Call the vendor to confirm before proceeding. Duplicate payments are very difficult to recover.

Step 4 — Cash Flow Monitoring

The Cash Flow tab gives you a real-time view of money coming in (inflows) and going out (outflows). This is the single most important daily view for managing Proalfa's liquidity.

Reading the Cash Flow Panel

  • Opening Balance: Today's available cash + bank balance (update manually or via bank reconciliation).
  • Expected Inflows (30 days): Receivables with due dates in the next 30 days — these are expected but not confirmed until they arrive.
  • Expected Outflows (30 days): Payables due, salary (from HR), TDS, GST, CC interest — confirmed obligations.
  • Projected Closing Balance: Opening + Inflows − Outflows. If this goes negative, raise it with management immediately.

When to Flag a Cash Shortfall

If projected closing balance is negative or below ₹5 lakh within the next 15 days: raise it with management that day. Do not wait. Options include: drawing down on the CC facility, chasing a large receivable, or deferring a payable with vendor agreement.

Updating Cash Flow Entries

1
Opening balance: At month start, enter the actual bank balance (sum of all accounts). Update mid-month if significantly different from projected.
2
Non-invoice inflows (e.g., loan drawdown, advance from client): Add under + Add Inflow with date, party, and amount.
3
Non-bill outflows (e.g., salary payout, GST payment, TDS deposit, petty cash): Add under + Add Outflow. Categorise correctly (Salary, Tax, Utilities, etc.).

Month-End P&L Checklist

  • Verify all sales invoices for the month are entered in Receivables
  • Verify all vendor bills for the month are entered in Payables
  • Enter salary payout (get total from HR after salary processing)
  • Enter GST net liability paid (from GSTR-3B computation)
  • Enter CC/OD interest for the month (from bank statement)
  • Enter any depreciation provision (check with CA)
  • Review Gross Margin % — if significantly lower than previous months, investigate cost overruns
  • Share P&L screenshot or PDF with management for review

Gross margin benchmark for PEB projects: A healthy gross margin for fabrication + erection contracts is typically 20–35%. If a month drops below 15%, there is likely a cost overrun or an under-priced project — review project-wise margins in the Projects tab.

Step 7 — Credit Line Management

The Credit Line tab tracks Proalfa's CC (Cash Credit) and OD (Overdraft) facilities with the bank. Mismanaging this is costly — bank interest is typically 10–14% p.a.

Key Metrics to Monitor Daily

MetricWhere to FindAction if Breached
Utilisation %Credit Line tab → Utilisation chartIf > 90% of limit: inform management; delay non-urgent payments; accelerate receivables collection
Available HeadroomSanctioned Limit − Current OutstandingIf headroom < ₹5L: do not draw further without management sign-off
Interest RateFacility agreement / bank communicationRenegotiate annually during renewal; compare with market rates
Renewal DateInsurance tab → Facilities sectionStart renewal 60 days before expiry; collect documents (audited balance sheet, IT returns, stock statement)

Recording a Drawdown

1
Draw from CC/OD: Transfer funds from CC account to current account in net banking. Note the amount and purpose (e.g., "Vendor payment — steel procurement").
2
Log in ERP: Credit Line tab → + Drawdown. Enter amount, purpose, date. This updates the utilisation chart automatically.
3
Record repayment: When receivable is collected and funds transferred back to CC account, log as Repayment with amount and date.

Best practice: Use CC/OD only for short-term mismatches (e.g., you need to pay a vendor today but client payment is expected in 15 days). Avoid using it as a substitute for working capital. Every rupee in CC costs interest — repay as soon as the receivable comes in.

Bank Submission Requirements (Periodic)

  • Stock Statement (monthly or quarterly): For CC facilities against stock, the bank requires a statement of raw material, WIP, and finished goods. Prepare and submit by bank's deadline (usually by the 7th of the following month).
  • Debtors Statement (monthly or quarterly): Aging of outstanding receivables. Cross-check with ERP Receivables tab before submitting.
  • Audited Financial Statements (annual): Required for renewal. Ensure your CA finalises accounts and provides audited P&L and balance sheet on time.

Step 8 — Insurance & Collateral Tracker

The Insurance tab tracks all insurance policies (fire, marine, machinery, workmen's compensation, etc.) and the bank collateral position (property mortgaged / pledged against credit facilities).

Insurance Policy Tracker

1
Adding a policy: Insurance tab → + Add Policy. Enter insurer name, policy number, type (Fire / Marine / WC / Machinery), sum insured, premium, start date, end date.
2
Renewal alerts: The tab shows a "Renewal Alerts" section for policies expiring in the next 60 days. Start the renewal process as soon as an alert appears.
3
Recording premium payment: When premium is paid, mark the policy as renewed in ERP and enter the new end date. Record the outflow in Cash Flow tab as well.

Renewal Procedure

  • 60 days before expiry: Contact broker/insurer for renewal quote
  • Compare premium with last year and market alternatives
  • Confirm sum insured reflects current asset value (especially for plant & machinery — get updated valuation if needed)
  • Get bank's endorsement letter if the policy is assigned to the bank (required for CC/OD collateral)
  • Pay premium before expiry — lapse in coverage is a serious risk, especially for fire and marine
  • File renewed policy document in Insurance File and update ERP
  • Send copy of renewed policy to bank if required for credit facility compliance

Bank Collateral Summary

The Insurance tab also shows the bank collateral position — property mortgaged, machinery hypothecated, and stock pledged against credit facilities. Keep this updated whenever:

  • A new credit facility is sanctioned or an existing one is modified.
  • A property mortgage is released or a new property is added.
  • The bank changes the terms of hypothecation (e.g., adds a new asset category).

Insurance lapse = bank default. Most CC/OD facilities require the borrower to maintain insurance on hypothecated assets and send proof of renewal to the bank annually. If you let a policy lapse, the bank can treat it as a covenant breach and recall the facility. Never let it happen.
